Work

Below are three poems from my manuscript, based on the life of Gilbert White, entitled In Defense of the Fern-owl.

In Which I Play Animal Vegetable Mineral with Uncle

Uncle wrote several intelligent people believe
star-sluch is frogs thrown up indigested
pray Molly
hunt for star-sluch
the daughter of Charles, he wrote
elder brother of the Reverend Andrew Etty here now
stares and wonders when I say it is vegetable
and Dr Chandler shakes his head
something about eating frogs about to reproduce
something about the egg-gelly swelling
in the craws of crows and herons
I beg of them to know why crows are not sometimes crop-sick
why they have not weak digestions in Hants as well as in Cheshire
we may just as well side with the old ones
and declare the trembling gelly
to be the excrement of stars
or the form stars take once fallen
uncle was glad of my reply
and to hear I met with the Star-sluch in Cheshire
so soon after examining the Tremella Nostoc in Hants
not that he’d had any doubt, he insisted
that the former was a vegetable
but intelligent people are still persuaded he lamented
this substance is a mass of indigested food
cast-up out of the stomachs of crows
they tell me so face to face
they say they have distinguished the limbs of frogs among it
and further their argument by telling me
the matter was found on the bough of an oak
one must not forget the seeds of all Fungi
are lighter than air (he made a line under all Fungi)
and therefore float about in it; and vegetate
when they happen to fall on a proper nest
pray Molly he wrote
apply a magnifying glass
to the substance and try to discover seeds

The Duke of Richmond's Moose

Michaelmas 1768
Foreign animals seldom fall in my way;
my little intelligence is confined to the narrow sphere
of my own observations at home.
at that time the wasps were thinning
and the hop-picking was nearly over
hedgehogs were boring into the grasswalks to eat the plantain roots
this was the year when nectarines rotted on the trees
the same morning I heard of the moose-deer
I remember rooks playing by pairs in the air over the church mead
and three black warty water-efts with fin tails and yellow bellies
were drawn up in our well-bucket
the moose had died the morning before
and was being kept in an old greenhouse
slung under the belly and chin by ropes
so to be in a standing position
long legs—short neck
ears vast and loping
as long as the neck
with a head like an ass
its lips were excessive
(a delicacy I am told)
its nostrils huge
I could not take measurements
could not examine the teeth
tongue, lips, hoofs etc. minutely
it was in so putrid a state
the stench was hardly supportable
and i had to stay clear of it

Outrage on Michaelmas

Here I hang-
specimen, oddity
limp and heavy in every direction
my four hoofs grazed by English grass
the pandemonious wilderness was my home
everything standing except that which had fallen
everything undone but that which held
I would have raged with a mightier effort
crossed all the rivers and the lakes too
crashed a million miles uphill
to escape this rotting in a sling
may my putrification
drive you clear off the world
I am for the moose-territories
the eternal bogs of Canada
my guardian pond-angels accompany me
with their blossomy upturned eyes and I am attached
by their magenta-twinged stems
to the bottom-muck of home
forgive me O squeamish beholder
if I begin to reek while your story plays out
